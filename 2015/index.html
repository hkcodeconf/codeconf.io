Hong Kong Code Conf

Hong Kong Code Conference will be held on Saturday 24th October 2015. It's a programming conference aimed at all programmers in Hong Kong. We want to share our skills and opinions. We want to inspire each other with our passion. We want to build a closer, stronger community.

The Speakers

  • Andrew Medworth

    Andrew Medworth graduated in Computer Science from Selwyn College, Cambridge in 2005, and moved from London to Hong Kong in 2010. His interests include programming languages, distributed systems and virtualization. He is currently helping to build a private cloud platform based on Docker. Github: @amdw
    Twitter: @medworth

  • Donal Ellis

    Donal is passionate about sharing knowledge. He spent many years in education, first as a computer science lecturer then as a software engineer, followed by a short detour into the world of startups. Now he is an enterprise support engineer for GitHub where he continues his passion of helping people build software better, together. Github: @donal
    Twitter: @nashape

  • Elaine Chan

    Java and Javascript dev in a bank. (Yes I know they are different!) Always curious and always procrastinating. Big fan of civic hacking and body hacking.

  • Godfrey Chan

After moving to Hong Kong, he has become a driving force in the startup community mentoring, helping and building the next generation of platforms, services and apps. Github: @dented
    Twitter: @dentedio

  • Holger Bartel

    Holger Bartel is a digital strategist & front end developer. He now runs the web design consultancy fooBartel Ltd. He also founded and runs the Open Device Lab HK, works as a part-time lecturer at The Hong Kong Polytechnic University's school of design, mentors for startups and loves speaking at conferences to share his thoughts. Github: @foobartel
    Twitter: @foobartel

  • Jack Chen

    Jack has devoted himself to Ruby development since he graduated. He is keen to open-source self-tested projects and give talks at conferences and Codeaholics. Having worked in different institutions (startups, 4A agency, travel company and web consultancy), he is happy to see technology bring productivity, efficiency and change the world. He is fond of Agile development, but also tries his best to be agile physically in terms of running, swimming and as a softball catcher! Github: @aquajach
    Twitter: @aquajach

  • Jonas S Karlsson

    Jonas S Karlsson, PhD, travelling Zen Hacker, likes do build new programming languages like pandalang.org and database kernels. Worked at Google building Google Megastore that underlies and scales Google App Engine consulting with numerous projects on how to scale to millions of users. Github: @yesco
    Twitter: @jonasskarlsson

  • Mart van de Ven

    Mart is the director of Droste, a Hong Kong-based Data Science Consultancy, a Data Science Instructor at General Assembly, Co-founder of Open Data Hong Kong, and the convener for Symbol & Key. Github: @tijptjik
    Twitter: @tijptjik

  • Matthew Rudy Jacobs

    A founder of Codeaholics, and Organiser of Hong Kong Code Conf, Matthew arrived in Hong Kong in 2010, and has been fighting to build a better developer community ever since. He works at GoGoVan and hopes that maybe you want to work there too ;). Github: @matthewrudy
    Twitter: @matthewrudy

  • Pedro Pimentel

    Pedro is a full-stack software & social engineer. He can seamlessly switch between talking code and talking with humans thanks to his experience working in consulting companies like Thoughtworks and ThoughtSauce. Originally from Brazil, he came to Asia to change the world and, moreover, change himself. Currently he's leading development teams at Wallem, a multi-national logistics company. Github: @zukunftsalick
    Twitter: @zukunftsalick

  • Tony Wong

    Tony Wong, co-founder and CTO of Shopline, is an entrepreneur and software engineer that has extensive experience in Web and Mobile Apps development. He is passionate about startups and is experienced in startup dynamics as a Founding Member of Viss, a mobile app focusing on fashion in Asia. Tony was an Overall Winner at Startup Weekend HK 2013 and he also holds a Master of Science from HKUST Business School. Prior to moving to Hong Kong, Tony has lived and worked in different parts of the world, including Latin America, Canada and other parts of South East Asia, giving him unique insight into many emerging markets around the globe. Github: @tonywongk
    Twitter: @tonywongk

  • Vincent Lau

    Javascript/Python/Scala guy. Work at a local start-up where my job is translating code into a world-class online translation platform. Love UX, Architectures, Data Science, Start-ups. Co-founded Code for Hong Kong, the civic hacking community. Github: @vincentlaucy
